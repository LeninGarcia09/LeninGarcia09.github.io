## Exam Overview

- **Exam:** AZ-305 — *Designing Microsoft Azure Infrastructure Solutions*
- **Certification:** Microsoft Certified: Azure Solutions Architect Expert
- **Level:** Expert (**requires AZ-104** first)
- **Role:** Solution Architect
- **Passing score:** 700 / 1000
- **Languages:** English, Spanish, Japanese, Korean, German, French, Portuguese (BR), Chinese (Simp/Trad), Italian
- **Cost:** price varies by country/region (~$165 USD in the US)

## Why This Exam Matters for AI Architects

AZ-305 validates the infrastructure and governance foundation that every AI solution depends on. AI workloads need networking (private endpoints, VNet), storage (ADLS Gen2), identity (Entra ID, managed identities), and governance (Azure Policy) — all AZ-305 domains, framed by the **Azure Well-Architected Framework** and **Cloud Adoption Framework**.

## Skills Measured (verified areas)

Per the official exam page, AZ-305 measures your ability to design across four areas. Weights change between versions — **[the official study guide](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/exams/az-305/) is the source of truth**:

- Design **identity, governance, and monitoring** solutions
- Design **data storage** solutions
- Design **business continuity** solutions
- Design **infrastructure** solutions

## How to Study (learning-science approach)

1. **Diagnose first** with the official practice assessment to expose weak domains.
2. **Design, don't memorize.** AZ-305 is a design exam — practice choosing services against requirements and constraints, then justify the trade-off (Well-Architected pillars).
3. **Active recall + spacing.** After each module, reconstruct the decision tree from memory; revisit across days (Dunlosky et al., 2013).
4. **Whiteboard case studies.** Draw reference architectures for identity, storage, BC/DR, and networking scenarios.
5. **Re-test to 80%+** on the practice assessment before booking.
