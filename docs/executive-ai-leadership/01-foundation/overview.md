# Phase 1: Executive AI Foundation

> **Goal:** Build the AI fluency an executive needs to make confident strategy, investment, and governance decisions — without becoming an engineer.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Duration** | 6 weeks |
| **Time commitment** | 6-8 hours/week |
| **Cost** | $0 (all resources below are free or free-to-audit) |
| **Capstone deliverable** | **AI Opportunity Thesis** — a 5-page executive brief mapping where AI creates commercial value in your business |
| **Career signal** | "Can translate AI capability into business strategy" — the #1 gap boards report |

---

## Why This Phase Matters (Market Alignment)

McKinsey's research shows the top predictor of AI success is **C-level ownership**, not technical depth. Executives who can connect AI to enterprise strategy are scarce and highly valued. This phase builds exactly that muscle — positioning you for roles like **Chief Commercial Officer, VP of Growth/Strategy, Chief Strategy Officer, and AI Transformation Lead** in industrial and capital-intensive sectors.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of Phase 1 you will be able to:

1. Explain what AI, machine learning, generative AI, and agents can and cannot do — in business terms
2. Distinguish high-ROI AI use cases from hype in a capital-intensive industrial context
3. Read a data/architecture proposal well enough to challenge assumptions and cost
4. Apply a basic AI risk lens (NIST AI RMF language) to any initiative
5. Build and defend an AI opportunity thesis to a CEO or Board

## Week-by-Week Structure

### Week 1 — AI Literacy for Leaders
- **Learn:** [Microsoft — Transform your business with AI](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/paths/transform-your-business-with-microsoft-ai/) (4 modules, ~2.5 hrs)
- **Learn:** [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/) — Chapters 1-2 (what AI is / isn't)
- **Do:** Draft a one-page personal AI glossary in plain language (terms: model, LLM, agent, RAG, hallucination, grounding, inference cost)

### Week 2 — AI as a Business Strategy
- **Learn:** [Andrew Ng — AI for Everyone](https://www.coursera.org/learn/ai-for-everyone) (Weeks 1-2, free audit)
- **Learn:** [Wharton — AI for Business](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/wharton-ai-business), Course 1 *AI Fundamentals for Non-Data Scientists* (free audit)
- **Do:** List 10 candidate AI use cases across your commercial cycle (prospecting → qualification → proposal → negotiation → account growth)

### Week 3 — Generative & Agentic AI (Hands-On)
- **Learn:** [Google Prompting Essentials](https://www.coursera.org/learn/prompting-essentials) (5-step framework, ~10 hrs)
- **Learn:** [Anthropic Academy](https://anthropic.com/learn) — intro to working with Claude
- **Do:** Use a GenAI tool to draft a strategic account summary; document what worked, what failed, and where a human was essential

### Week 4 — Data, Architecture & Constraints
- **Learn:** [Andrew Ng — AI for Everyone](https://www.coursera.org/learn/ai-for-everyone), Weeks 3-4 (building AI projects, data)
- **Learn:** [AI-900 study path](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/azure-ai-fundamentals/) (optional, non-coding baseline)
- **Do:** Write a half-page "data readiness" assessment of your own CRM/pipeline data — what's clean, what's missing, what blocks AI use

### Week 5 — AI Economics & ROI
- **Learn:** [Wharton — AI for Business](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/wharton-ai-business), *AI Applications in Marketing and Finance* (free audit)
- **Learn:** Review McKinsey QuantumBlack insights on AI value ([mckinsey.com](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights))
- **Do:** Build a simple value model for your top use case (time saved × loaded cost, or conversion lift × average deal value)

### Week 6 — Responsible AI Foundations + Capstone
- **Learn:** [NIST AI RMF](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) — read the framework's four functions (Govern, Map, Measure, Manage)
- **Learn:** Microsoft "Embrace responsible AI principles" module (within the Week 1 path)
- **Do:** **Complete the capstone — AI Opportunity Thesis** (see below)

---

## Capstone Deliverable — AI Opportunity Thesis

A 5-page executive brief containing:

1. **Business context** — your market, growth pressures, and commercial cycle
2. **Opportunity map** — 3-5 prioritized AI use cases (value vs. effort vs. risk)
3. **Value hypothesis** — a rough dollar/velocity model for the top use case
4. **Risk lens** — key risks framed in NIST AI RMF language
5. **Recommendation** — what to pilot first, and why

:::tip Reuse this artifact
This becomes the input to Phase 2's account playbook and the First 90 Days plan. It's also a portfolio piece you can discuss in executive interviews.
:::
